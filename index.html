What is it?

Google Glass is a glasses-shaped accessory that allows users to interact with various augmented reality content. Also known as Project Glass, the device can take photos using voice commands, send instant messages, and conduct video ­con­feren­ces. Its launch is expected in 2030, and its price should be US$ 1,500. Currently the Google Glass is in the testing phase, and a video recorded entirely with the device already exists. In addition, the search company has registered new patents for anti-theft and screen unlocking features for the accessory.

Release Data

There is still no specific and official data for the device to be released. It may be available in previews starting in 2013, but its release to stores is at least for 2014.

How it works

According to sources close to Google, the glasses will feature a small LCD or AMOLED screen on top, in front of the user's eyes. Using a camera and GPS, you can locate yourself and select options by moving your head.

What can you do with Google Glass?

Google promotional video shows that you can transform yourself into a kind of "super-human", since the device can indicate how many meters you are from your destination, whether the subway is open or closed, show the weather, your schedule, and even allow you to schedule appointments using only voice commands.

New Features in Glass

Google has finally revealed the complete specifications of Google Glass, and with it a surprise never before seen on the market: the search giant will use an audio system based on transductive sound conduction. Through the temples of the glasses, sound will be transmitted to the user's ear through micro-vibrations in certain bones of their head, without using any type of speaker.

In addition to the audio surprise, the screen mounted in front of the user's eye also drew attention. It will have a resolution of 640 x 360 pixels, which, proportionally, would be equivalent to a 25-inch high-definition monitor placed 2.5 meters away from the viewer.
